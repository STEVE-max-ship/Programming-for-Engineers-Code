PGE Exam Problem

Ball Games Court

Quido and Hugo want to build a temporary court for their ball games in the university backyard during summer holidays.

They divided the backyard into a rectangular grid, each cell of the grid is either empty or it contains a tree. Two of the trees will serve as a poles and there will be a net held between them. For that reason they decided to call each tree a pole.

The court itself will be rectangular area in the grid, consisting of some number of cells. The borders of the court will be parallel to the borders of the grid and the court will contain whole cells only.

The two poles on which the net will be held, let us call them net poles, will be chosen in a particular way:
Both net poles will be situated in the same row of the grid, this row is called the net row.
The number of court cells on one side of the court has to be equal to the number of cells on the other side of the court with respect to the net row. Putting it simply: The net row divides the court exactly in the middle.
The position of the cells with net poles will be adjacent to the court but not inside the court.

The court, of course, will not contain any poles. No part of the court can be situated outside the grid.

Given the conditions listed above, the friends want to choose the net poles cells in such way that will allow them to maximize the area of the court. The area of the court is equal to the number of grid cells in it.

In the following example schemes, empty cells, cells with poles, and cells of the court are represented by dots, O's, and X's, respectively. (Spaces between cells are added for better readability.)
Note that there may be more poles adjacent to the court, not only the net poles. Such configuration does not violate the conditions given above.
Also note, that in extreme cases the court may consist of only the cells between the net poles, in the net row.

In the Example, the grid is depicted in the first scheme, the remaining five schemes represent various possible location of the ball game court in the grid. The maximum area of the court is 12 cells, it is reached in the last scheme.

The task

For the given court scheme, find the maxumum possible area of a ball games court.

Input

The first input line contains two positive integers R, C separated by space. They represent, in this order, the number of rows in the grid and the number of columns in the grid.
Next, there are R lines of input, each contains C values separated by spaces. Each line contains values of the corresponding grid row. Empty cells are represented by zeroes, cells with poles are represented by ones.
The size of the grid is at most 1000 × 1000.

Output

The output contains one text line with one integer: The maximum possible area of a ball games court built in the given input grid.
It is guaranteed that the output area is always positive in the problem data.
