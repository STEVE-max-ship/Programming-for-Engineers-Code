Micronation Flag Design

pdf version (Download and rename with .pdf extension.)

Quido is designing a flag for his newly established micronation located somewhere in Dejvice, Prague.

The flag is rectangular and it is covered by stripes. It is divided by into the left part, denoted LP, and the right part, denoted RP. Both LP and RP are rectangular. Each part is divided into stripes. LP is divided into vertical stripes RP is divided into horizontal stripes. The width of all stripes in LP is the same and the width of all stripes in RP is the same. However, the widths of stripes in LP and RP may differ.

Each stripe is completely filled with a color. There are two colors, C1 and C2. The colors of stripes in both parts are alternating. In LP, the leftmost stripe is filled with C1, the next stripe is filled with C2, the next stripe is filled again with C1 etc. In RP, the topmost stripe is filled with C1, the next stripe immediately below it is filled with C2, the next stripe is filled again with C1 etc.

The task

Produce the image of the flag, given its numerical description. The flag is represented as a 2D array, in which stripe colors C1 and C2 are represented as different integer values. All measures are given in pixels, each pixel is represented by one array cell.

Implementation Note

You may prepare a complete "image" of the flag internally in an array and only print it out completely at the end of your code. Or, you may also print the flag line by line, without any more complex internal representation.

Input

The input consists of one text line containing seven positive integers separated by spaces, FH, FW, LPW, LSW, RSW, C1, C2. FH and FW are Flag Height and Flag Width, respectively. LPW is Left part width. LSW and RSW are Left Stripe Width and Right Stripe Width, respectively. C1 and C2 are the colors stripes, C1 is the color of the leftmost stripe in LP and topmost stripe in RP. It is guaranteed that the width of LP is a positive multiple of LSW, and also the height of the flag (height of RP) is a positive multiple of RSW. Values of C1 and C2 are ranging from 0 to 9.
The size of the flag does not exceed 800 × 800 pixels.

Output

Print the flag according to the input specification. Each pixel of the flag is filled with either C1 or C2 color. Each pixel is represented as one cell of a 2D array which contain only C1 and C2 values. Each pixel row is represented as one text line in the output, there are no spaces between adjacent cells, neither horizontally, nor vertically.
